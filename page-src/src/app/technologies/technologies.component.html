My favourite toys

Backend

Java is the language I speak the most, and Spring my favourite 'accent'. Kind of like being in my hometown and speaking Spanish, but hey, I like to get out of my comfort-zone, and some Python scripts have fallen into my hands from time to time. However, I'm always eager to learn new languages.

The dependency management tool of my choice is Apache Maven. I like to use it even when dealing with frontend dependencies, if it's plausible, although certain occasions call for other (not less fancy) tools like npm or Bower.

Frontend

I started off with ActionScript3, back then when it was popular, Adobe Flex (currently Apache Flex), was my bfast, lunch and dinner for about 4 years (comes without saying that I liked to 'spice it up' with some Parsley).

After this sweet time, came a JavaScript/HTML transition. Swimming all over an ocean of tools, I stumble upon one framework that made me feel, somewhat, warm inside. AngularJS was my choice.

Nowdays, I play with google's new toy Angular ('2' how it's still referred to), and personally, TypeScript fills me up with joy when coding frontend.
